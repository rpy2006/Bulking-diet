OR: 2 slices bread + 1 tbsp peanut butter
OR: banana shake (banana + milk + honey)

06 Supplement Guide

Food always comes first. Supplements are optional additions — not replacements. Focus on getting your diet right before spending here.

Must Have
Creatine Monohydrate
3–5g daily (any time)
Most researched supplement for strength & muscle gains. Affordable (₹500–800 / month). Take consistently every day.
Must Have
Vitamin D3
1,000–2,000 IU daily
Most Indians are deficient. Critical for testosterone, immunity, and muscle function. ₹150–300 / month.
Optional
Whey Protein
1 scoop (25g) post-workout
Convenient if you struggle to hit protein goals. Not necessary if diet is on point. ₹1,500–2,500/kg. Try MuscleBlaze or Nakpro.
Optional
Omega-3 (Fish/Algae)
1–2g EPA+DHA daily
Reduces inflammation, supports joint health & muscle recovery. If vegetarian, choose algae-based. ₹300–600/month.
Optional
Multivitamin
1 tablet daily with food
Covers micronutrient gaps in a budget diet. Choose Revital H or Centrum. ₹200–400/month. Not a substitute for vegetables.
Skip
Pre-Workout / Fat Burners
Not recommended for beginners
Waste of money at your stage. Bananas + black coffee is a free, effective pre-workout. Fat burners don't work without a calorie deficit.
07 Common Mistakes to Avoid
🚫
Not eating enough protein
The #1 mistake beginners make. You must hit 120g+ protein daily. Without it, you cannot build muscle no matter how hard you train. Track your food for the first 2–4 weeks.
🚫
Skipping the post-workout meal
Your muscles are in a catabolic state after training. The 30–60 minute window is when protein and carbs are most effective. Missing this meal is leaving gains on the table.
🚫
Eating too little or too much (dirty bulk)
Too little = no muscle gain. Too much = excess fat gain. A lean bulk requires a controlled +200–300 kcal surplus. If you're gaining more than 0.5kg/week, cut back slightly.
🚫
Changing diet plans every week
Nutrition works over weeks and months, not days. Stick to a plan for at least 4–6 weeks before evaluating results. Constant switching prevents any real progress assessment.
🚫
Not sleeping enough
Muscle is built during sleep, not in the gym. Aim for 7–9 hours every night. Poor sleep reduces testosterone, increases cortisol, and kills muscle-building — no diet plan can fix this.
🚫
Ignoring vegetables and micronutrients
Vitamins, minerals, and fibre are essential for hormones, recovery, and digestion. Add at least 1–2 portions of vegetables (sabzi/salad) at every major meal.
🚫
Drinking too little water
Dehydration reduces strength, focus, and nutrient absorption. Minimum 3–4 litres daily, more on training days. Don't rely on thirst as a cue — drink consistently throughout the day.
08 Expected Timeline
Reality Check

As a natural beginner, you're in the best position to gain muscle — your body responds very well to new training stimulus. Expect 1–1.5 kg of lean muscle per month in the first 6 months if diet and training are consistent. This slows down after the beginner phase (~6–12 months).

Weeks 1–4 · Month 1
Neural Adaptation & Foundation
Your body learns to fire muscle fibres efficiently. Strength increases rapidly (20–40% lifts) even before visible muscle. Scale may not move much. Focus on consistency, form, and hitting your calories.
+0.5–1 kg on scale (mostly glycogen + water)
Months 2–3
Visible Muscle Development Begins
You'll start noticing fuller muscles, especially in chest, shoulders, and arms. Friends and family may notice. Strength gains continue rapidly. Body fat should remain controlled on a lean bulk.
+1–1.5 kg lean muscle added
Months 4–6
Significant Body Transformation
Noticeable physique change. You should have added 3–4 kg total (muscle + some fat). Your maintenance calories will increase as you gain mass — slightly increase food intake if weight gain stalls.
+2–3 kg lean muscle (cumulative)
Months 7–12
Solid Foundation Built
You'll have transitioned out of "beginner" status. Gains slow slightly but are still excellent. Consider a 4–6 week mini-cut if body fat has crept up. Then continue bulking.
+4–6 kg lean muscle (full year)
Year 1–2
Intermediate Physique
With consistent diet and training, you can expect to go from 57 kg to 63–67 kg with significantly improved body composition. After 2 years of proper training, your physique will be dramatically different.
+6–10 kg lean muscle total
Key Principle to Engrave in Your Mind

Muscle is built with protein + surplus calories + progressive overload in the gym + sleep. The diet plan above handles the first two. Show up to the gym 4× per week, sleep 8 hours, and be patient. Consistency over 6 months beats perfection for 2 weeks. You've got everything you need — execute.

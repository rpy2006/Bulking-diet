07Mistakes to Avoid
Not eating enough protein
You must hit 120g+ protein daily. Track food for the first 2–4 weeks.
Skipping the post-workout meal
The 30–60 min window is when protein and carbs are most effective. Missing it leaves gains on the table.
Eating too little or dirty bulking
Too little = no gain. Too much = fat gain. Keep your surplus controlled at +200–300 kcal.
Changing plans every week
Stick to a plan for at least 4–6 weeks before evaluating results.
Not sleeping enough
Muscle is built during sleep. Aim 7–9 hours. Poor sleep raises cortisol and kills gains.
Drinking too little water
Minimum 3–4 litres daily. Use the water tracker to stay accountable.
08Expected Timeline
Weeks 1–4
Neural Adaptation & Foundation
Strength increases rapidly before visible muscle. Focus on consistency and form.
+0.5–1 kg (glycogen + water)
Months 2–3
Visible Muscle Development
Fuller muscles in chest, shoulders, arms. Strength gains continue rapidly.
+1–1.5 kg lean muscle
Months 4–6
Significant Body Transformation
Noticeable physique change. 3–4 kg total added. Increase food if weight stalls.
+2–3 kg lean muscle (cumulative)
Months 7–12
Solid Foundation Built
Out of beginner phase. Consider a mini-cut if body fat has crept up.
+4–6 kg lean muscle (full year)
Year 1–2
Intermediate Physique
57 kg → 63–67 kg with dramatically improved body composition.
+6–10 kg lean muscle total
Key Principle

Muscle is built with protein + surplus calories + progressive overload + sleep. Show up to the gym 4× per week, sleep 8 hours, and be patient. Consistency over 6 months beats perfection for 2 weeks.
